★ Why this home is exceptional
Priced correctly, 5824 Rudolph is one of the most valuable properties on Anastasia Island right now — and the market is moving away from it every month it sits.
This is a rare 5-bedroom, 3.5-bath oceanside home with panoramic Atlantic and Intracoastal views from all three floors, on a quiet dead-end street, one block to the surf, with no HOA and no CDD. It was meticulously remodeled in 2019 with no expense spared. The anchor comp — 5730 Rudolph, same street — went pending in just 15 days at $742/sqft, and is 700 sqft smaller than the subject. The blended 6-comp model lands the subject's fair value at ~$1.60M, while the anchor $/sqft alone signals closer to $2.0M — meaning the pricing strategy carries real upside if you execute the aggressive / bidding-war play. Since the owner's 2021 purchase at $1.028M, the micro-market has run at an implied ~9.3% CAGR, and the appreciation curve continues to outrun the price the home would attract on a slow, aspirational list. The right move is to price for velocity, not patience. A tightly priced list at the aggressive end of the band should see multiple offers in the first two weeks and clear at or above the implied market value.

How the model works

1. Comp normalization. Each Paragon comp's sale or list price is converted to $/sqft, then time-adjusted forward to today using a fixed Anastasia Island appreciation rate of 9% / yr. (That rate is calibrated to the actual 9.3% CAGR observed on the subject property between its 2021 purchase at $1.028M and today's modeled value of ~$1.60M.) A sale that closed 6 months ago is bumped up by ~4.4%, a sale from 12 months ago by 9%, etc.

2. Implied market value. The mean of those adjusted $/sqft figures is multiplied by the subject's 2,720 sqft to produce a single blended fair-value number. You'll see it in the "Implied Value (live model)" cell at the top of the Subject Property panel and in the metrics row of the Pricing Strategy section. Edit any cell in the comp table and this number recalculates instantly.

3. Single dynamic strategy panel. The Pricing Strategy section uses one slider that runs from −15% (loss-leader) to +35% (golden price). As you move the slider, the recommended list price, $/sqft, expected days-on-market, expected offer count, likely close price, and the strategic narrative all update in real time. The panel's color and the heading change to reflect which "zone" the slider is in:

• −15% to −10% · Loss-Leader — fastest sale, but you give up real money
• −10% to −5% · Aggressive — bidding-war play, recommended starting position
• −5% to +3% · At Market — clean sale, no drama, no upside
• +3% to +10% · Market Plus — slightly above the comp band, normal cycle
• +10% to +22% · Aspirational — patient list for the right cash buyer
• +22% to +35% · Golden Price — lottery ticket, long DOM, not recommended

4. Outcome heuristics (DOM, offer count, close price haircut) are calibrated to recent Anastasia Island Area 07 second-home behavior: aggressive lists at >6% under fair value typically clear at or above the implied market in 7–21 days; lists at 15%+ over fair value typically take 6+ months and need at least one price cut, with the eventual close usually 5–15% below list.

5. Anchor signal vs blended model. The blended 6-comp model lands at ~$1.60M today. The single best one-to-one comparable — 5730 Rudolph (same street, same Butlers Beach pocket, pending in 15 days) — implies closer to $2.0M when you scale its $742/sqft up to the subject's 2,720 sqft. Wherever you set the slider, that $2.0M anchor signal is the upper guardrail to keep in mind: it's the reason the aggressive play is genuinely aggressive, not foolish.
